Bonding Session 2.0
Date: 14th November 2022.

Time: -: 8:00 am to 11:00 am

Venue: - Marine Drive (Sunday Street)

No. of Attendees: - 78 Leos

No of beneficiaries – Community at large


Leo Club of SIES had their second bonding session 2.0 at Marine Drive (Sunday Street) on the 14th of November 2022.

A Sunday morning at Marine Drive is a perfect way to bond with your fellow members outside your college premises. We started our event with our PR Head, Leo Rishi Kapoor giving a brief introduction about why we had gathered there. To warm up a bit we started the event with some fun games and activities. For the first game, we asked everyone present out there , members as well as nonmembers to form a big circle, and played the game of ‘passing the ball’ with the music playing in the background. When the music stops the person who had the ball at that time had to perform something they liked, Everyone enjoyed this with great enthusiasm. For the second game, we played ‘Simon Says’ which worked to help wake up the brain-body connection. We divided everyone into different groups to make the event more fun to play. Post games session, we sat down and jammed to some amazing songs sung by the Music Band of the Leo Club of SIES . It was enjoyed not only by the members but also by the passersby who loved and appreciated it. Some even sang along with us. We even played some light games of hula hoops and football with the kids out there.It was a fun as well as an interactive bonding session. The event ended with the vote of thanks from our Secretary Leo Joanne George. One of the most significant benefits of bonding sessions with members is that they help to strengthen relationships among team members. When team members interact with each other outside of work, they get to know each other on a personal level, which helps to build trust and understanding. This, in turn, can lead to better communication and collaboration during work-related tasks Bonding sessions with members can also encourage creativity. When team members are relaxed and comfortable with each other, they are more likely to share ideas and brainstorm creatively. This can lead to new insights and solutions that might not have been possible in a more formal work setting.
